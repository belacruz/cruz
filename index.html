You do not rise to the level of your goals, you fall to the level of your systems

Goals are good for setting a direction but systems are best for making progress. achieving a goal is only a momentary change. If you dont change your habits, than after reaching a goal, you will slip into your old patterns.

Winners and losers have the same goals. We think we need to change our results, but results are not the problem, we need to change the system that causes that result.

Fix the inputs and the outputs will fix themselves

If we think about an ice cube in a room at -10°C degrees, it will not melt since the temperature is bellow 0. As we start to increase the heat nothing seems to change at first, even when the room is already at -1°C the ice cube still looks the same. Finally when the temperature in the room reaches 1°C the ice begins to melt. What happened is that, even tho the progress was not making any big difference at first, it was accumulating and being stored, at one point the progress finally tips the balance.

The same happens to progress in life, at first nothing seems to change, but after some time doing an expecific activity, the balance tips and the results start coming fast. People will call it an overnight success, but in reality, it took time and effort.

Unfortunately it creates what we call "valley of disappointment", where our expectations about what should happen meet what actually happens, and we are left in a void of progress for a time before the results come.

"When nothing seems to help, i go and look at a stonecutter hammering away at his rock, perhaps a hundred times without as much as a crack showing in it. Yet at the hundred and first blow it will split in two, and I know it was not that last blow that did it, but all that had gone before."

Goals vs Systems

Your commitment to the process determine your progress.

The "either or" situation

Goals create an "either or" situation: Either you achieve your goal or you fail and are a disappointment. You're not happy until you achieve your goal, and when you finally achieve it, soon you find yourself empty and sad because you don't have another goal, or because it wasn't good enough. It makes no sense to restric your satisfaction to one scenario when there are many paths to success.

Systems-first mentality

Goals are at odds with long term progress. When all your hard work is set after a goal, what is left after you achieve it? A systems-first mentality provides the antidote. When you fall in love with the process rather than the product, you don't have to wait to give yourself permission to be happy. You can be satisfied anytime your system is running. And a system can be successful in many different forms, not just the one you first envision.

Find the right system

If you're having trouble changing your habits, the problem isn't you. The problem is your system. Bad habits repeat themselves again and again not because you don't want to change, but because you have the wrong system for change.
